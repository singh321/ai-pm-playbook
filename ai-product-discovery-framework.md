# AI Product Discovery Framework

A practical framework for discovering the right AI product opportunities.

---

## Why AI discovery is different

AI product discovery is not just about asking users what they want.

Users may ask for:
- “an AI assistant”
- “chat with data”
- “automate everything”

But these requests are often too broad.

Good AI discovery requires translating vague interest into:
- real workflow pain
- measurable value
- feasible product shape
- trusted user experience

---

## My AI product discovery framework

I use 5 steps:

1. Identify workflow pain
2. Find the right AI leverage
3. Map trust and control requirements
4. Define MVP shape
5. Define success metrics

---

## 1. Identify workflow pain

Start with the workflow, not the model.

### Questions
- What job is the user trying to get done?
- Where is the workflow slow, repetitive, noisy, or cognitively heavy?
- What part causes delay, drop-off, or frustration?
- Which tasks happen often enough to matter?

The best AI opportunities usually sit inside repeated workflow pain.

---

## 2. Find the right AI leverage

Once pain is clear, ask where AI adds leverage.

Common AI leverage types:
- summarize
- extract
- classify
- generate
- prioritize
- recommend
- orchestrate
- enrich

### Key question
What part of the workflow becomes meaningfully better with AI?

---

## 3. Map trust and control requirements

Before shaping the product, define:
- what users must verify
- what users can delegate
- what the system should explain
- where human approval is needed

This step prevents over-automation too early.

---

## 4. Define MVP shape

A good AI MVP is usually narrower than teams first imagine.

### Good MVP traits
- focused use case
- clear workflow placement
- measurable quality
- manageable risk
- obvious value moment

### Bad MVP traits
- generic chat for everything
- unclear target user
- vague success criteria
- no eval path

---

## 5. Define success metrics

Before building, define:
- adoption metrics
- quality metrics
- trust / workflow metrics
- business metrics

This keeps discovery tied to real outcomes.

---

## Discovery interview questions for AI products

### Workflow understanding
- Walk me through how you do this today.
- What takes the most time?
- Where do you lose context?
- What feels repetitive or mentally heavy?

### AI opportunity mapping
- Where would assistance save the most time?
- Where would recommendations help?
- Where would you still want manual control?

### Trust understanding
- What kinds of mistakes would be unacceptable?
- What would make you trust this system?
- What would make you stop using it?

---

## Final principle

The best AI discovery does not start with:
> “Where can we add AI?”

It starts with:
> “Where does the workflow break, and where can AI create trusted leverage?”
